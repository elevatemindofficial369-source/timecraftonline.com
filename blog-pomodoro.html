History of the Pomodoro Technique

Francesco Cirillo, an Italian university student, struggled with focus. In the late 1980s, he started experimenting with a tomato-shaped kitchen timer. He discovered that 25 minutes of focused work followed by a short break dramatically improved his productivity. He named it "Pomodoro" after the timer.

The Science Behind Pomodoro

Multiple studies support this technique:

  • University of Illinois study (2011) showed short breaks improve focus.
  • DeskTime research found top 10% productive employees work in ~52-minute sprints.
  • Ultradian Rhythm research by Nathaniel Kleitman proves our brain works in 90-minute cycles.

Basic Pomodoro Technique – Step by Step

  1. Choose one clear task
  2. Set timer for 25 minutes
  3. Work with full concentration
  4. Take 5-minute break
  5. Repeat 4 times → then take 15-30 min long break
